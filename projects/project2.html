Fire detectoin

problem Statement

IR fire detection system is expensive compared to regular fire detection system. High-volume-low-margin manufacturing sectors with higher risk of fire probably(...more research needed) doesn't adopt IR fire detection system. Example of those sectors include: textile & garmet factories, food processing & grain storage, plastic rubber manufacturing. Computation has become cheaper and there are many foundational models trained to finetune for rudimentary fire detection system to deplay fast and inexpensively.

Solution

Inexpensive non-ir camera live fire detection using smaller computer vision model finetuned to detect fire in large manufactoring facilities where smoke alarms are less effective due to the size. Fine-tune a lightweight vision classification model using labeled data to predict images containing fire.

    • Result

    Potential Cost Reduction: A fraction of the cost of IR-based fire detection systems. Real-time Detection: Faster response compared to smoke alarms in large spaces. Scalability: Easily deployable across multiple manufacturing plants with minimal changes.

    limitations

    Only the finetuning part has been completed and the system is not finished Environmental Sensitivity: Accuracy may be affected by lighting, reflections, and smoke dispersion. False Positives: Requires ongoing model improvements to distinguish between actual fire and similar visual patterns. Computational Constraints: Low-power edge devices may need optimizations for real-time processing. Regulatory Compliance: May require certification or approval in fire safety regulations before large-scale adoption.
